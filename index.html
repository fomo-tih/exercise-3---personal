Appliance Energy Consumption in Australia

Energy usage of common appliances plays a major role in household electricity bills. For example:

  • Refrigerators: Average 200–600 kWh per year depending on size and efficiency.
  • Televisions: Small LED TVs use ~60 kWh/year, while large plasma TVs can exceed 350 kWh/year.
  • Air Conditioners: Range between 300–2000 kWh per year based on capacity and climate.

Choosing energy-efficient models with a higher star rating can save Australian households hundreds of dollars annually. This also lowers overall demand on the national electricity grid.
